Investigators of the OS4LS Full Application "BIDS 2.0+ ecosystem:
AI-Ready Life-Science Data Standard". Publications cited across all
biographies are aggregated in the References section at the end and
referenced by number in each biography. In each reference, names of
this proposal's PI or Co-PIs are shown in **bold**.

## 1. Yaroslav O. Halchenko, Ph.D. — Principal Investigator (Dartmouth)

**Role in proposal:** Principal Investigator (Applicant); overall
coordination; BIDS specification, schema, and bids-utils.

**Current position:** Research Professor of Psychological and Brain
Sciences (with adjunct appointment in Computer Science) and Founding
Director of the Center for Open Neuroscience, Dartmouth College.

**IDs:**
Email yaroslav.o.halchenko@dartmouth.edu ·
[ORCID 0000-0003-3456-2493](https://orcid.org/0000-0003-3456-2493) ·
[GitHub yarikoptic](https://github.com/yarikoptic) ·
[Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=EbtfZcwAAAAJ) ·
[centerforopenneuroscience.org](https://centerforopenneuroscience.org/)

**Biography.** Yaroslav Halchenko is Research Professor of
Psychological and Brain Sciences at Dartmouth College, where he
founded and directs the Center for Open Neuroscience. His work sits
at the intersection of neuroimaging, data standards, and reproducible
research infrastructure. He is an Emeritus Steering Group member of
the Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS) and was one of five named
recipients of the 2023 Neuro-Cooper Foundation Open Science
International Prize awarded to the BIDS Steering Group. He leads
DataLad (distributed data management), ReproIn (heuristic
DICOM-to-BIDS conversion), and the ReproNim reproducibility center's
containers effort, and serves as Co-Investigator on DANDI,
OpenNeuro, and EMBER. He also created and maintains NeuroDebian.
This combination of long-standing BIDS governance, hands-on
maintainership of the specification and tooling stack, and deep ties
across the open-neuroscience archive ecosystem uniquely positions
him to lead the BIDS 2.0+ effort.

**Selected relevant publications:** [1], [2], [3], [4], [5].

**Selected open-source contributions:** BIDS specification and
ecosystem (Emeritus Steering Group; maintainer of the specification,
schema, and downstream converters/validators); DataLad
(https://www.datalad.org) — creator and lead of the distributed
data-management system built on Git and git-annex, used across
BIDS/DANDI/OpenNeuro for versioned dataset publication and reuse;
ReproIn (https://github.com/ReproNim/reproin) — heuristic-driven
DICOM-to-BIDS conversion built on HeuDiConv;
///repronim/containers — DataLad-distributed collection of
reproducible BIDS-App / neuroimaging containers; NeuroDebian
(https://neuro.debian.net) — long-running Debian-based platform
packaging neuroscience software (co-founder / maintainer since 2006);
DANDI Archive — Co-Investigator on the NIH BRAIN Initiative archive
for neurophysiology (NWB) data; PyMVPA (co-author); HeuDiConv
(maintainer).

**Role in proposal:** Co-PI (Stanford lead); BIDS specification,
bids-validator, PyBIDS, OpenNeuro integration.

**Current position:** Albert Ray Lang Professor of Psychology (and,
by courtesy, of Psychiatry and Behavioral Sciences); Department
Chair of Psychology; Associate Director, Stanford Data Science;
Director, Stanford Center for Reproducible Neuroscience and SDS
Center for Open and Reproducible Science, Stanford University.

**IDs:**
Email russpold@stanford.edu ·
[ORCID 0000-0001-6755-0259](https://orcid.org/0000-0001-6755-0259) ·
[GitHub poldrack](https://github.com/poldrack) ·
[Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=RbmLvDIAAAAJ) ·
[poldracklab.org](http://poldracklab.org/)

**Biography.** Russell A. Poldrack is the Albert Ray Lang Professor
of Psychology at Stanford University, where he chairs the Department
of Psychology, serves as Associate Director of Stanford Data
Science, and directs the Stanford Center for Reproducible
Neuroscience and the SDS Center for Open and Reproducible Science.
His research uses cognitive neuroscience to study decision-making,
executive control, and learning, with a parallel program focused on
the infrastructure and methods needed for reproducible, transparent,
open science. He established the OpenfMRI project in 2009, which
became OpenNeuro, now the primary open archive for neuroimaging
data. He is a co-founder of the Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS)
and an Emeritus member of its Steering Group, and originated the
Cognitive Atlas ontology. His group develops widely used open-source
tools including fMRIPrep, MRIQC, PyBIDS, and the bids-validator, and
has led community efforts to standardize reproducible neuroimaging
workflows.

**Selected relevant publications:** [1], [6], [2], [7], [8].

**Selected open-source contributions:** BIDS specification
(co-founder; Emeritus Steering Group member); OpenNeuro (PI; founded
as OpenfMRI in 2009); bids-validator and PyBIDS (funder,
contributor, Stanford host of core maintainers); fMRIPrep and MRIQC
(co-developer via Stanford Center for Reproducible Neuroscience);
Cognitive Atlas (founder) — formal ontology for cognitive
neuroscience; Neurosynth (co-founder) — automated meta-analysis of
the neuroimaging literature.
